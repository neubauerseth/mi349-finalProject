# mi349-finalProject
This is my Final Project for my MI 349 Class

# Requirements
### Your final project must include the following:

- A brand and full prototype created in Figma (min 4 pages)
- Four (4) fully valid HTML5 pages
- One separate, linked, fully valid CSS document for your styles
- Two images (relative link, source must be within your project)
- Thoughtful copywriting (see rubric below; concise, clear writing that communicates the goals of the site to the visitor)
- No spelling or grammatical errors
- Thoughtful design that follows the MI 349 Design Principles (see rubric below)
- The design should be intentional.
- The design should have a clear style and color palette
- The final site should be responsive and render correctly on mobile devices
- All source code must be in a new GitHub repository
- The final site must be hosted on Netlify

# Rubric
### The final project will be graded out of 400 points.

### Point Breakdown

- Figma brand (50 points)
- Figma prototype (50 points)
- 4 Validated HTML5 pages (40 points)
- Separate CSS stylesheet (10 points)
- Image requirement (20 points)
- Mobile friendly (40 points)
- Spelling and Grammar (20 points)
- Github and Netlify hosting (20 points)
- Copywriting (quality scoring) (50 points)
- Your writing will be subjectively graded based on quality; here's what I'm looking for:
    - Did you communicate your ideas in a simple, clear way?
    - Is your writing applicable to your audience?

- Here are some good tips and guidelines [link](https://digital.gov/topics/usability/)
- Design (quality scoring) (100 points)
- Your design will be subjectively graded based on quality; here's what I'm looking for:
    - Does your design show effort and forethought?
    - Is it esthetic (does it follow the class design principles)?
    - Does it accomplish the goals of the site?
    - Does it use a common CSS layout technique?
- I will be fair with subjective grading and I will apply the same standards across all projects. If you have any issues with the subjective portion of your grade, you can always reach out for clarification.
